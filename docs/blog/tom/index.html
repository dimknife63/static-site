Why Tom Bombadil Was a Mistake

< Back Home

"Old Tom Bombadil is a merry fellow; bright blue his jacket is, and his boots are yellow."

In the vast weave of J.R.R. Tolkien's legendarium, there exists a curious anomaly: Tom Bombadil. I, as an ancient Archmage, must assert that his inclusion in The Lord of the Rings was, unfortunately, a narrative misstep.

An unpopular opinion, I know.

An Intriguing Yet Disjointed Figure

  1. An Unnecessary Interlude: The encounter with Tom serves as a temporal diversion that detracts from the urgency of the quest.
  2. An Outlier in Purpose: His escapades add little to the central narrative.

An Enigma that Remains Unresolved

  • A Mystery Without Resolution: Unlike other enigmatic figures, Tom remains shrouded in mystery.
  • A Departure from Tone: His presence contrasts sharply with the solemnity of the saga.

Conclusion

Tom Bombadil was a narrative anomaly — a whimsical reflection in the mirror of Middle-earth's grand narrative.
